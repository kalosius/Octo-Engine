The Rich History of ST.Francis Senior Secondary School

Our Heritage

ST.Francis Secondary School was founded in 1909 through a joint effort between the Buganda Kingdom and the Church Missionary Society United Kingdoms, led by Venerable Rev. Harry Bowers and Ssingo County Chief Owek. Paul Nsubuga Bakunga. Initially a boys' school, it has undergone several transformations, including the construction of its first permanent building on 27th March 1937, promotion to ST.Francis Secondary School status, and the admission of the first female students. On 30th July 1947, it was recognized by British Prime Minister Winston Churchill for its contributions to cotton production during World War II. The first science laboratory was opened by Sir Andrew Cohen on 10th October 1953, followed by the inauguration of the Boys' Hostel and the primary section's new site by the King of Buganda on 6th September 1958. It became a government-aided secondary school in 1978, attained Advanced Level status in 1982, and marked its 85th anniversary with the opening of the Old Students’ Library by King Ronald Muwenda Mutebi II on 5th October 1994. The computer center and main hall were opened in 1999 by the Minister of Education and Sports to celebrate the 90th anniversary, leading up to the centenary celebration on 19th September 2009
